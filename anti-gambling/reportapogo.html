Contact the Relevant Authorities

There are several channels to report suspected illegal POGO operations:

1. PAGCOR

  • Reach out to PAGCOR to report unlicensed or non-compliant POGOs.

    Hotline: (+632) 8521-1542 / (+632) 8522-0299

    Email: info@pagcor.ph

2. Philippine National Police (PNP)

  • For criminal activities related to POGOs, contact your local police station or the PNP Anti-Cybercrime Group.

    Hotline: 117 or 911

    Cybercrime Unit: acg@pnp.gov.ph

3. Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE)

  • For concerns about labor violations, especially involving foreign workers.

    Hotline: 1349

4. Bureau of Immigration (BI)

  • For issues involving undocumented foreign workers or overstaying POGO employees.

    Hotline: (+632) 8465-2400

    Email: xinfo@immigration.gov.ph

Submit Anonymous Reports

If you are concerned about your safety, you can report anonymously through official government websites or hotlines. Be honest and accurate in your report to help expedite investigations.

Philippine Offshore Gaming Operators (POGOs) have become a significant part of the local economy, but they also bring challenges, including potential illegal activities, public disturbances, and unregulated operations. If you suspect a POGO is operating illegally or causing harm in your community, reporting it to the authorities is crucial for accountability and public safety.

Why Reporting Matters

Illegal POGOs harm the community and undermine the integrity of the country’s regulatory framework. Reporting these activities helps:

  • Protect public safety and community well-being.
  • Ensure taxes and licenses are appropriately enforced.
  • Hold accountable those engaging in illicit activities.

How to Report a POGO

  1. Gather Information

    Before reporting, collect as much information as possible to help authorities investigate:

    • Business name and location.
    • Nature of the suspected illegal activity (e.g., unlicensed operation, disturbances, criminal activities).
    • Evidence such as photos, videos, or documentation, if safe to obtain.

  2. Contact the Relevant Authorities

    Reach out to the appropriate government or law enforcement agencies to file a report.




Protecting your community


Illegal POGOs and their associated activities can harm communities, strain local resources, and tarnish the country's reputation. By reporting these activities, you play a vital role in ensuring a safer and more regulated environment for everyone.

Your vigilance can make a difference. If you see something suspicious, don’t hesitate to act.
