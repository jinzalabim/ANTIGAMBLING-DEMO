MANAGING YOUR GAMBLING ADDICTION

Recognize the Signs

Recognizing the signs of gambling addiction is crucial in taking the first step toward recovery. Common indicators include:

  • Increased time spent gambling, even when you don’t want to.
  • Gambling despite negative consequences, such as financial losses.
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to cut down on gambling.
  • Lying about gambling behavior or hiding it from loved ones.

Steps to Regain Control

Taking control over gambling addiction requires a multi-step approach. Here are some steps to regain control:

  1. Admit that you have a problem and seek help.
  2. Set limits on your gambling activities, such as time and money limits.
  3. Avoid triggers, like places or situations where you used to gamble.
  4. Seek professional help, such as therapy or support groups, to address underlying issues.

Developing Long-Term Strategies

Long-term strategies are essential for maintaining recovery from gambling addiction. Consider the following:

  • Building a strong support network with family, friends, and professionals.
  • Engaging in healthy hobbies and activities to replace gambling.
  • Monitoring progress and seeking help when temptations arise.
  • Commitment to self-care and mindfulness to manage stress and emotions.

YOU ARE NOT ALONE.

Gambling addiction can feel isolating, but help is available. Taking steps to address it is a sign of strength. With the right support and tools, you can break free from gambling addiction and build a fulfilling, healthy future.
