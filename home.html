The Game-ification of Online Casinos in the Philippines: Risks, Resources, & Recommendations

Rationale:

Online casino advertisements have become prolific whether it be on social media or TV networks. In the Philippines, this situation is made even worse when famous actors, influencers, vloggers, and the like, endorse online gambling. When people like Ivana Alawi or Alden Richards endorse online gambling — it’s as if it validates the existence and negates the risk of online casinos. Online casinos are everywhere, and they require little to no set-up or registration to be able to play, and the same goes for creating them.

Inspired by these online casino websites, we wanted to show how easy it is for regulated online casinos and online gambling websites by PAGCOR to implement a warning for users when a certain amount of time has already passed while gambling. We also want to show how the government can effectively combat the effect of gambling addiction by actually putting safeguards that can redirect them easily to online gambling addiction resources.

To have these types of pop-ups interrupt play time means that users are able to know when too much is already too much, and give them avenue to recognize if they are prone to addiction. In addition, if we do not put these resources for people within their constant reach and let them recognize it might be harmful, they might have a hard time to be the ones to find the resource to help themselves.

Relation to Cyberspace, Organizations, and Communities:

Cyberspace


The proliferation of online casinos highlights the role of cyberspace as a platform for various activities, including gambling. The ease of access and minimal setup required to engage with these platforms demonstrates how digital spaces can facilitate both recreational and harmful behaviors.

Organizations


The Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR) serves as the regulatory organization responsible for overseeing gambling activities. However, the current scenario shows a gap in implementing responsible gambling measures. The proposed website prototype positions itself as a model for how organizations like PAGCOR can enhance their online presence and accountability by integrating user warnings, time checks, and redirection to addiction resources. It emphasizes the role of organizations in actively safeguarding user welfare while fulfilling their regulatory mandates.

COMMUNITIES


Communities, both digital and physical, are significantly impacted by the gambling epidemic. Celebrity endorsements add a layer of normalization that can mislead vulnerable individuals. By providing immediate access to educational resources and support, our website aims to ensure that communities have the tools to recognize and address gambling addiction effectively.

This site is made as a final requirement for our class in Cyberspace, Organizations and Communities (OSDCYOC) A52.
