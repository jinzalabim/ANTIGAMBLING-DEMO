Gambling

Gambling is an act of staking money on a game's or event's unpredictable outcome. It can be found in different forms, including the stock market, bingo, casinos, and many more. In recent years, the most popular type of gaming has been slot or poker machines. This act of gambling can result in a vicious cycle of addictive gambling.

POGOs

POGOs are online gaming companies that cater to foreign clients, primarily offering casino-like services through digital platforms. They are regulated by the Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR), which ensures compliance with laws, licenses, and tax obligations.

While many POGOs operate legally, some engage in illicit practices such as:

  • Operating without a valid license.
  • Involvement in criminal activities such as money laundering or human trafficking.
  • Disrupting local communities with noise, traffic, or safety concerns.

However, in pursuant to Executive Order No. 74, all POGOs and offshore gaming operations nationwide are banned, including the application and renewal of their licenses. The closure of these operations is scheduled for December 31, with all PAGCOR-issued licenses "deemed canceled" by that date.

PAGCOR

The Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR) is a state-owned agency regulating and operating gaming activities in the Philippines. Established in 1977, it was created to curb illegal casinos and support national socio-economic goals.

Key Roles

  • Regulate and license: Casino gaming and other games of chance.
  • Generate revenue: For government programs and projects.
  • Promote tourism: Enhance the country’s appeal through gaming hubs.

Income Allocation

  • 5% franchise tax to BIR.
  • 50% of net revenue to the National Treasury.
  • Funds Universal Health Care and sports programs.
  • Support for host cities’ development projects.
